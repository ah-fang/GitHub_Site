Respite

Steady, steady, feel it rumbling,
Turn your fingers into stone,
Spark new trust upon the granite,
Loose your tension with a groan.
Sink your knees into the topsoil,
Rest your chaos-weary head,
In the weeping wooden thicket
(How it feared that you were dead!)
Let it feel your flame so vital
That it banishes all doubt.
Sleep now, darling. Rest. Recover.
Only time shall bring you out.

Suspicion

Would they change, beloved, knowing
Of the turning of the sands?
Would they blanch like lightless saplings?
Or recoil from your hands?
Do they let their wantings wander
From their loudest daily needs?
Dare they raise their heads from squabbles
Over cottages and weeds?
Could their eyes behold a coming
Of an age beyond our years?
Did the shrill and distant singing
Reach the edges of their ears?

That this spirit cannot claim
